Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib

The Defender of the Prophet's Legacy, The Paragon of Justice and Courage, The Gatekeeper to the City of Knowledge, The Martyr of Islam.

Life of Amir al-Mu'minin Imam Ali (AS):

  • 601 CE:- Imam Ali was born in Mecca into the Banu Hashim clan, which was a prominent tribe in Arabia.
  • 610 CE:- Imam Ali converted to Islam at the age of 10, becoming the first male to accept the message of Islam after the Prophet Muhammad.
  • 622 CE:- The Prophet Muhammad and Imam Ali migrated from Mecca to Medina in an event known as the Hijra, which marked the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
  • 624 CE:- Imam Ali participated in the Battle of Badr, which was the first major battle between the Muslims and the Meccans, and was a significant victory for the Muslims.
  • 625 CE:- Imam Ali married Fatimah, the daughter of the Prophet Muhammad, and they had several children, including Hasan and Husayn.
  • 627 CE:- Imam Ali participated in the Battle of the Trench, which was a siege of Medina by the Meccans that the Muslims successfully defended against.
  • 628 CE:- The Treaty of Hudaibiya was signed between the Muslims and the Meccans, with Imam Ali as the Prophet's representative in negotiations.
  • 632 CE:- The Prophet Muhammad passed away, and Imam Ali was appointed as his successor by a group of Muslims who recognized his leadership qualities and loyalty to the Prophet. He became the fourth Caliph of Islam.
  • 657 CE:- The Battle of Siffin took place between Imam Ali and Muawiyah, which lasted for several months and ended in a truce that left the issue of leadership unresolved.
  • 658 CE:- The Battle of Nahrawan took place, where Imam Ali fought against the Kharijites, a group that had broken away from the Muslim community.
  • 658 CE:- Imam Ali was given the title of "Haider-e-Karrar" by the Prophet Muhammad, which means "lion of God" and is a symbol of Imam Ali's bravery and loyalty to Islam.
  • 659 CE:- Imam Ali's forces successfully captured the fortress of Khaibar, which was a stronghold of the Jewish community in Arabia.
  • 661 CE:- Imam Ali was assassinated in Kufa by a Kharijite named Ibn Muljam.

"Imam Ali is the foundation of knowledge, the door to the city of wisdom, and the most knowledgeable of the Companions concerning the Book of Allah and the Sunnah of His Prophet."

-- Muslim scholar, Ibn Taymiyyah
